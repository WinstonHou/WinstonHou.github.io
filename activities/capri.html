CAPRI

A policy research project on semiconductor supply chains, TSMC’s U.S. expansion, and Taiwan’s economic sovereignty.

Role: Policy Research Intern
The Assignment

What needed to be solved

I investigated how U.S. semiconductor policy and TSMC’s Arizona expansion could affect Taiwan’s economic security and the resilience of the global chip supply chain.

My Role

What I contributed

  • Synthesized policy, trade, and supply-chain research into a focused argument.
  • Examined the tension between geographic diversification and Taiwan’s strategic leverage.
  • Produced an independent research paper and refined it through feedback from CAPRI researchers.
Reflection

What I learned

The project showed me that systems can appear efficient while remaining fragile. A single bottleneck, policy shift, or geopolitical disruption can expose dependencies that an optimized model hides.
